The voices, the visions and the history of modern rap culture

Look Through the History of Rap by going through this

The Old School (1979 – 1983)

Elements: Rapping, beatboxing, scratching, and sampling. Key Artists: Sugarhill Gang: Their track “Rapper’s Delight” was the first hip-hop record to gain widespread popularity, sampling Chic’s “Good Times.” Grandmaster Flash and The Furious Five: Delivered socially conscious messages in tracks like “The Message.”

The Golden Age (1983 – 1997)

Innovations: Advances in recording technology. Notable Artists: Run-DMC: Fused rock and hip-hop. Public Enemy: Known for politically charged lyrics. LL Cool J: Influential solo artist. N.W.A: Shaped gangsta rap. A Tribe Called Quest: Introduced jazz-infused hip-hop.

The Conscious Wave (1983 – 2000)

Themes: Social consciousness, introspection. Notable Artists: De La Soul: Known for their positive messages. Common: Blended conscious lyrics with soulful beats. The Roots: Fused live instrumentation with rap.

The East vs. West (1991 – 1997)

Conflict: Rivalry between East Coast (New York) and West Coast (Los Angeles) artists. Key Figures: Tupac Shakur (2Pac): Represented West Coast. The Notorious B.I.G. (Biggie Smalls): Iconic East Coast rapper.

The Rise of Southern Rap (1990 – 2005)

Southern Influence: Artists from Atlanta, Houston, and New Orleans gained prominence. Notable Artists: OutKast: Blended Southern flavor with innovative production. Lil Wayne: Dominated the 2000s with his mixtapes.

The Bling Era (1997 – 2006)

Themes: Materialism, luxury, flashy lifestyles. Key Artists: Jay-Z: Symbolized bling culture. 50 Cent: Known for his debut album “Get Rich or Die Tryin’.”

The Blog Era (2006 – 2014)

Digital Shift: Rise of online platforms for music discovery. Key Players: Kid Cudi: Influential in blending rap and alternative sounds. Drake: Dominated the charts during this era.

The Rise of Trap (2014 – present)

Trap Influence: Dominant sub-genre characterized by heavy beats. Prominent Artists: Future: Pioneered trap music. Migos: Known for their triplet flow.
